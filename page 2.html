Identifying Mislabeled Images in Supervised Learning Utilizing Autoencoder

Supervisor: Prof. Andrew Whinston
Committee Members: Prof. Risto Miikkulainen, Prof. Gordon S. Novak Jr.
Bachelor's Honors Thesis, Sep 2020 ~ Dec 2020
Department of Computer Science, University of Texas at Austin, Austin TX

  • Introduced a method to identify and remove mislabeled images in the training dataset.
  • Obtained MNIST, Fashion MNIST, and Cifar 10 datasets, generated noise samples ramdomly.
  • Utilized convolutional autoencoder to reduce the image dimension and maximize the features.
  • Applied DBSCAN algorithm to identify outliers as noise samples. Successfully identified 70% of the noise samples in the datasets.


Learning Latent Subspaces in Camera ISP Variational Autoencoders

Supervisor: Prof. Chandrajit Bajaj
Moncrief Summer Internship, Jun 2020 ~ Nov 2020
Oden Institute for Computational Engineering and Sciences, University of Texas at Austin, Austin TX

  • Implement a multi-filter Gaussian Mixture Variational Auto-Encoder for images denoising.
  • Data: Obtain images from CelebA-HQ dataset, manually generate three types of noises.
  • Autoencoder: Build an encoder that projects image-data to latent space, then build multiple decoders that reconstruct images.
  • Clustering: Cluster the data in the latent space, determine which decoder to use based on the clusters.
  • Evaluation: Use PSNR, SSIM and UQI to evaluate the quality of the images that are reconstructed from the decoders.

Approximating Boolean Functions with Disjunctive Normal Form

Supervisor: Prof. Anna Gal
Analysis of Boolean Function (Graduate-Level Research-Based Course), Jan 2020 ~ May 2020
Department of Computer Science, College of Natural Science, University of Texas at Austin, Austin TX

  • Discussed the universal upper bound size of disjunctive normal form (DNF) that can approximate Boolean functions.
  • Showed several examples of common used Boolean functions and argued that the universal bound can be applied to these examples, and specific Boolean functions may have more optimal bounds.
  • Parity and Majority: did survey on approximating Parity functions and Majority functions using DNF and showed the bound of DNF size is within the universal bound.
  • Monotone function: proved the upper bound of the size of DNF that can approximate monotone Boolean functions.

H-1B Visa Status Classification and Classifier Analysing

Supervisor: Prof. Angela Beasley

Undergraduate Research and Writing (Research-Based Course), Jan 2020 ~ May 2020
Department of Computer Science, College of Natural Science, University of Texas at Austin, Austin TX

  • Classifier Performance Analysis through H-1B Visa Certification Status Classification.
  • Data Exploration: explore how individual features (employer, wage, worksite, etc.) related to visa certified rate using Sklearn.
  • Feature Engineering: remove highly correlated fields, standardize numerical data and one-hot-encode categorical data.
  • Classification: train data using Random Forest, Nearest Neighbors, SVM, MLP, Logistic Regression, and AdaBoost. Predict the results and analyze the performance of models.
  • Integration: explore voting classifier and stacking classifier, integrate seven classifiers listed above, achieve 91%+ accuracy, 90%+ precision and 99%+ recall.
